## üìÑ Research Papers

### Core Self-Healing Network Papers

**1. "Self-Healing in Mesh Networks: Concepts and Implementations"**
- Where to find: IEEE Xplore (https://ieeexplore.ieee.org)
- Search terms: "self-healing mesh networks", "autonomous network recovery"
- What I'll learn: Failure detection, rerouting strategies, performance metrics

**2. "Topology Control in Wireless Ad Hoc and Sensor Networks"**
- Authors: Paolo Santi (2005)
- What it covers: Foundational work on dynamic topology management
- What I'll learn: Power control, connectivity maintenance, distributed algorithms

**3. "A Survey of Survivability in Multi-Hop Wireless Networks"**
- Where to find: IEEE Communications Surveys & Tutorials
- What it covers: Comprehensive overview of network resilience techniques
- What I'll learn: Fault tolerance, recovery mechanisms, QoS preservation

**4. "Self-Healing Techniques for Wireless Mesh Networks"**
- Where to find: ACM Digital Library (https://dl.acm.org)
- Search: "self-healing wireless mesh"
- What I'll learn: Proactive vs reactive healing, distributed vs centralized approaches

**5. "Resilient Overlay Networks"**
- Authors: David Andersen et al. (MIT, 2001)
- What it covers: Pioneering work on routing around failures
- What I'll learn: Path diversity, failure recovery latency

### Optimization & Topology Design

**6. "Optimization of Wireless Mesh Network Topology"**
- Where to find: Journal of Network and Computer Applications
- What I'll learn: Simulated annealing for network design, cost functions

**7. "Topology Design and Optimization of Wireless Mesh Networks"**
- Where to find: Computer Networks journal
- What I'll learn: Multi-objective optimization, placement algorithms

**8. "Network Topology Optimization with Simulated Annealing"**
- Where to find: Various engineering journals
- Search: "network topology optimization" + "simulated annealing"
- What I'll learn: Energy functions, cooling schedules, constraint handling

### Routing in Dynamic Networks

**9. "Ad Hoc On-Demand Distance Vector (AODV) Routing"**
- RFC 3561
- URL: https://www.ietf.org/rfc/rfc3561.txt
- What it covers: Standard protocol for dynamic routing
- What I'll learn: Route discovery, maintenance, and repair mechanisms

**10. "Dynamic Source Routing (DSR)"**
- RFC 4728
- What it covers: Alternative approach to adaptive routing
- What I'll learn: Source routing and route caching techniques

### IoT & Practical Applications

**11. "Internet of Things: Wireless Sensor Networks and Self-Healing"**
- Where to find: IoT journals and conferences
- Search: "IoT self-healing networks"
- What I'll learn: Energy efficiency, scalability, real-world deployments

**12. "Self-Configuration and Self-Healing in Wireless Sensor Networks"**
- Where to find: ACM/IEEE conferences on sensor networks
- What I'll learn: Autonomous operation, distributed healing

## üí° Questions I Ask While Reading

When going through papers, I focus on:

1. **What triggers self-healing?** (failure detection mechanisms)
2. **How fast can it recover?** (latency of rerouting)
3. **What's the overhead?** (extra messages, computation costs)
4. **How does it scale?** (10 nodes vs 1000 nodes)
5. **What are the failure assumptions?** (random failures vs targeted attacks)
